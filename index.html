About Me

I am a landscape ecologist who uses trans-disciplinary techniques to study the ecology, ecosystem services, and management of ecosystems. My team is involved in ecosystem assessments, ecosystem service prioritization, environmental change impact studies, and livelihood maximization.

Our studies are conducted in different landscapes- forests, plantations, urban landscapes, and wetlands. Using different socio-ecological frameworks and earth observation methodologies, we focus on unravelling ecosystem ecological processes, resources, inherent challenges of the Anthropocene era, and how best to prioritize their use and conservation.

Research Focus

Sustaining Ecosystems, Ecosystem Services and Livelihoods (SEEL)

My current work focuses on providing insights into the ecology of understudied ecosystems, their potentials to address climate change issues through carbon sequestration, as well as their capacity for other ecosystem services, and the needed strategies to optimize such services for ecological processes and livelihoods.

This initiative is conducted in natural ecosystems and more recently in urban areas and other landscapes. Considering the growing threat of environmental change, this initiative focuses on both natural and man-made ecosystems and how best their ecological functions could be harnessed to address sustainable development goals.
