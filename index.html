About Me

My research spans biogeography, ecology, biodiversity dynamics, conservation, and environmental management. I focus on understanding variations in ecosystems, their ecological processes, and the extent to which land use and climate change drive forest modification, ecosystem function, and carbon sequestration.

Much of my work has centered on wetlands, particularly freshwater swamp forests in the Niger Delta, where baseline ecological data are scarce. I am now extending this research to other ecosystems to explore how land use change affects biodiversity, carbon storage, and ecosystem services that sustain local livelihoods.

I am actively involved in university teaching, supervision, and collaborative research, and a member of the British Ecological Society and the Association of Nigerian Geographers.
